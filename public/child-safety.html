Postervia

Child Safety Standards

Last updated: May 13, 2026

Postervia is a city companion and community app for local guidance, social planning, stories, and user-to-user communication. Postervia is not directed to children. We maintain a zero-tolerance policy for child sexual abuse and exploitation (CSAE) and child sexual abuse material (CSAM).

These standards apply to all Postervia users, posts, comments, messages, profiles, media, links, and any other content or behavior connected to the Postervia service.

Our CSAE standard

Postervia prohibits any content or behavior that depicts, promotes, facilitates, solicits, normalizes, or attempts to enable child sexual abuse or exploitation. This includes real, fictional, computer-generated, AI-generated, or edited content involving minors in sexual or exploitative contexts.

For these standards, a minor means any person under 18 years old.

Prohibited content and conduct

  • CSAM, including images, videos, screenshots, links, or instructions related to sexual abuse or sexual exploitation of minors.
  • Grooming, sexual solicitation of minors, sextortion, coercion, trafficking, or attempts to move a minor to unsafe communication channels.
  • Sexualized comments, roleplay, fantasies, requests, or descriptions involving minors.
  • Content that identifies, targets, endangers, blackmails, or harasses a minor.
  • Attempts to share, request, sell, buy, trade, or preserve CSAM or CSAE-related material.
  • Any evasion of moderation, reporting, age safeguards, or account restrictions related to child safety.

In-app reporting

Users can report safety concerns from inside Postervia. Open the relevant post, comment, chat, profile, or other item, tap the report or overflow menu, and submit a report. If a dedicated child safety category is available, choose it; otherwise describe the concern clearly in the report text.

Urgent child safety reports can also be sent to our designated child safety contact below. Reports involving possible CSAM, grooming, exploitation, or immediate risk to a minor are prioritized for urgent review.

Review and enforcement

When Postervia receives a child safety report or detects possible CSAE, we may take one or more of the following actions:

  • Remove or restrict access to the content.
  • Suspend, disable, or terminate the account involved.
  • Preserve relevant records where legally appropriate.
  • Escalate the report for human review and safety response.
  • Report confirmed or suspected CSAM or CSAE to appropriate regional, national, or law-enforcement authorities where required or appropriate.

Prevention and moderation

Postervia uses a combination of community rules, user reporting, automated checks, and manual review to reduce the risk of abusive or illegal content. We review serious safety reports as a priority and may update these standards as our product, moderation tools, or legal obligations change.

Child safety laws

Postervia complies with applicable child safety laws and cooperates with lawful requests from competent authorities. We do not permit users to create, upload, distribute, request, or promote CSAM or CSAE on Postervia.
